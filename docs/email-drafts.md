# School Outreach Email Templates

These are reusable templates for cold sales outreach to high schools and the districts that serve them. Primary audience: CTE directors, STEM/curriculum coordinators, principals, superintendents, and the teaching-and-learning, career-readiness, and grants leads listed in `docs/contact-collection.md`.

Use these as starting points only. Personalize the first line, send a real human follow-up, and never overstate FAA pass rates, school adoption, grant eligibility, or guaranteed career outcomes.

## How to Use

- **Length target.** First-touch emails should stay under 100 words. Follow-ups should stay under 60. Busy school administrators skim on mobile.
- **Structure.** One personalized line. One value line. One short ask. One link maximum.
- **One ask per email.** Either ask for a reply, ask for a routing referral, or ask for a 15-minute call. Not all three.
- **Pick one angle.** Safety, Creative, STEM, career pathway, or funding. Do not stack angles in a first email.
- **Send from a real person.** Always sign with a name. Never use "team" signatures for first-touch.

## Our Three Tracks

Use the named tracks consistently across emails so they become recognizable:

- **Safety Track** — FAA Part 107 preparation, airspace awareness, safe flight habits, risk planning, and responsible operation.
- **Creative Track** — drone photography, videography, storytelling, mapping, and portfolio-quality aerial media projects.
- **STEM Track** — flight systems, electronics, weather, data, problem solving, and applied engineering habits.

Students can mix tracks. Teachers can assign one track or build a full pathway.

## Strengths to Highlight

Lead with these. They are real differentiators against generic drone bundles or one-off enrichment activities.

- **Three named tracks** that map to how schools already think (safety, creative, STEM).
- **FAA Part 107-aligned** content with structured units, sub-units, and practice.
- **Progress tracking and org accounts** so teachers and program leads can see student progress at the course and unit level.
- **Flexible delivery** — runs as a teacher-led classroom course, a hybrid course, or fully self-paced online.
- **Built by the drone community.** The course is written by people who care about safe, competent operators, not just a checkbox certificate.
- **Room to go beyond the minimum.** Students who get hooked can explore creative media, career applications, and deeper STEM topics in the same platform.
- **Career-connected.** Students see how the same skills appear in real industries: media, real estate, construction, agriculture, inspections, public safety, and aviation.

## Tone Guidance

- **Workforce-focused districts:** lead with safety, FAA preparation, employability, compliance, and teacher-led delivery.
- **Innovation-focused districts:** lead with applied STEM, creative production, flexible access, and student interest.
- **When unsure:** use balanced language — career readiness, safety, STEM, flexible delivery, and student interest.

Avoid: hype words ("revolutionary", "transform"), false urgency, vague jargon ("synergy", "future-ready"), and anything that sounds like an AI-generated press release.

## Follow-Up Templates

Send the first follow-up `4-6 days` after the initial email. Subsequent follow-ups: `10-14 days`, then `21-28 days`. Stop after the polite close unless they reply.

Best,
James

## Personalization Snippets

Drop one of these into the first sentence so the email feels human:

- I saw your CTE program includes [program] and thought drones would extend that pathway.
- I noticed [School] has a STEM/robotics/aviation focus and wanted to share a related course.
- I saw your students publish [yearbook/media/news], and our Creative Track pairs well with that work.
- I noticed [School/District] is investing in career readiness and wanted to share a drone option.
- I saw [grant/news/program] and thought a flexible drone pathway may fit upcoming planning.
- I noticed your role covers [curriculum / CTE / STEM / teaching and learning] and wanted to ask about drone curriculum.

## Length & Format Reminders

- First-touch body: aim for `60-100 words`, three short paragraphs, one link, one ask.
- Follow-up body: aim for `30-60 words`.
- Avoid bullet lists in cold emails — they look templated.
- Never send attachments on first contact.
- Never include more than one URL in the body. The signature can carry the consultation link.
- If you would not say it out loud to a teacher, do not send it.
