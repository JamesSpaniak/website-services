# School outreach workflow

This is the first operating playbook for school outreach. Start manual, learn from real replies, and automate only the parts that become repetitive enough to justify it.

Primary audience: schools, districts, CTE directors, STEM leads, career academies, community colleges, education departments, grant/program offices, and regional education service organizations.

Primary goal: book qualified conversations for Drone Edge school programs, then move interested buyers toward a clear quote, contract package, and launch plan.

## Manual-first workflow

Use a spreadsheet or lightweight CRM as the source of truth. Keep research, list approval, outbound messages, replies, scheduling, and quote prep human-owned at first.

1. Pick a weekly target segment.
   Examples: Pennsylvania CTE directors, STEM coordinators in neighboring states, community colleges with aviation or workforce programs, districts with drone clubs, schools pursuing STEM/CTE grants.
2. Build a batch of 25 to 50 researched contacts.
   Prefer smaller, accurate batches over broad scraping. Confirm the school, role, email, and why the contact is relevant.
3. Review the list before sending.
   Remove weak fits, duplicate domains, generic inboxes that should not receive a personalized message, and contacts outside the current region or segment.
4. Send the first-touch email manually or through an approved mail-merge.
   Personalize the first sentence. Link to the school page and curriculum page. Use the consultation page as the default call to action.
5. Track every send and reply the same day.
   Every contact should have a status, next follow-up date, owner, and notes field.
6. Follow up on a fixed cadence.
   Use short, useful follow-ups. Do not invent urgency or overstate funding, pass rates, or school adoption.
7. Route interested replies to scheduling.
   Either send the consultation link or propose two to three times manually. Capture program size, timeline, funding, and decision process before the call when possible.
8. Run the qualification call.
   Confirm program type, number of students, tracks needed, implementation timing, procurement path, and legal/security requirements.
9. Send recap and next steps within one business day.
   Include links, answers to open questions, and a clear next action: pilot, quote, follow-up call, or not a fit.
10. Move qualified opportunities to quote/proposal.
    Use the one-year or three-year package checks before sending anything formal.

## Contact sourcing

Prioritize roles that own curriculum, CTE, STEM, workforce, grants, or school program decisions.

Good first contacts:

- CTE director or coordinator.
- STEM director or coordinator.
- Career academy director.
- Aviation, robotics, engineering, computer science, or media production teacher.
- Workforce development lead.
- Community college continuing education or aviation program lead.
- Superintendent or assistant superintendent for curriculum.
- Principal at schools with visible CTE/STEM programs.
- Grants coordinator or business office contact, especially after interest is confirmed.

Suggested research sources:

- District staff directories.
- School board agendas and CTE program pages.
- State CTE directories.
- Intermediate unit, BOCES, regional service center, or education service agency directories.
- Community college workforce program pages.
- SkillsUSA, robotics, aviation, media production, and STEM competition pages.
- Public grant award announcements for STEM, CTE, aviation, robotics, or workforce programs.

Do not rely on stale third-party lists without verification. Public directories change, and school staff turnover is common.

## Funnel stages

Keep stage names simple so reporting stays useful.

| Stage | Exit criteria |
| --- | --- |
| Researched | Contact and source URL found. |
| Ready | Fit reviewed and approved for outreach. |
| Emailed | First email sent. |
| Follow-up active | No reply yet, but next follow-up is scheduled. |
| Replied | Human reply received. |
| Meeting booked | Time agreed or consultation submitted. |
| Qualified | Need, timeline, buyer path, and program fit confirmed. |
| Quoted | Quote or proposal package sent. |
| Won | Agreement accepted or payment/PO process underway. |
| Lost | Not a fit, no budget, timing wrong, or competitor chosen. |
| Nurture | Good fit, but not active this cycle. |

- New contacts researched.
- Contacts approved for outreach.
- First-touch emails sent.
- Follow-ups sent.
- Replies received.
- Meetings booked.
- Qualified opportunities.
- Quotes sent.
- Wins/losses.

## Email cadence

Default cadence:

- Day 0: first touch.
- Day 4 to 6: short follow-up.
- Day 10 to 14: funding or curriculum angle.
- Day 21 to 28: polite close or nurture.
- After reply: respond within one business day.

Stop outreach when someone asks not to be contacted, when the contact is clearly not relevant, or when the school has a different requested process.

### First touch

Subject ideas:

- Drone curriculum for `[School/District]`
- FAA Part 107 pathway for `[Program Name]`
- CTE/STEM drone course question

Template:

```text
Hi [Name],

I saw [personalization: CTE/STEM/aviation/media/workforce program detail] and wanted to ask whether [School/District] is exploring drone curriculum for students.

Drone Edge helps schools run structured FAA Part 107 prep and applied drone tracks through a browser-based platform with student progress tracking, org accounts, practice exams, and flexible async or hybrid delivery.

A quick overview is here:
https://thedroneedge.com/schools

Curriculum details are here:
https://thedroneedge.com/schools/curriculum

Would it be worth a short conversation to see if this fits your program or upcoming funding cycle?

Best,
James
Drone Edge
https://thedroneedge.com/consultation
```

### Follow-up 1

```text
Hi [Name],

Just following up on my note about Drone Edge for school drone programs.

The short version: schools can use it for FAA Part 107 preparation, drone video/photo, and AI + drones tracks, with org-level progress visibility for teachers or program managers.

If this is not your area, is there someone on the CTE, STEM, or workforce side I should ask instead?

Best,
James
```

### Funding / CTE angle

Use only when relevant. Do not imply grant eligibility is guaranteed.

```text
Hi [Name],

One reason I thought this might be relevant is that drone programs often fit the way schools describe STEM, CTE, workforce, and industry credential pathways.

We keep a short page with funding-language considerations and official PA SMART context here:
https://thedroneedge.com/schools/funding

Eligibility always depends on the specific grant, district process, and allowable costs, but the page may help frame the conversation with a grants or curriculum team.

Would you be open to a 30-minute call?

Best,
James
```

### Polite close

```text
Hi [Name],

I do not want to keep filling your inbox, so I will close the loop for now.

If drone certification, CTE, STEM, media production, or AI + drones becomes relevant for your students, the school overview is here:
https://thedroneedge.com/schools

Happy to reconnect when timing is better.

Best,
James
```

## Scheduling process

For warm replies, either send the consultation link or propose times directly.

Use the consultation link when the prospect is early-stage:

```text
Thanks, [Name]. The easiest next step is a free 30-minute consultation:
https://thedroneedge.com/consultation

If it is easier, send me a couple of times that work and I can coordinate by email.
```

Use manual scheduling when the reply is already specific:

```text
Thanks, [Name]. I can do [Option 1] or [Option 2] ET. If neither works, send a couple of windows and I will work around your schedule.

Before the call, it would help to know:
- Rough student count.
- Whether this is CTE, STEM, media, AI/CS, workforce, or another track.
- Target semester or school year.
- Any funding or procurement deadline.
```

Before the call, capture:

- School or district name.
- Primary contact and role.
- Program type.
- Expected student count.
- Desired start date.
- Current drone hardware, if any.
- Tracks of interest.
- Decision makers.
- Funding source or budget status.
- Procurement/legal/security requirements.

## Qualification checklist

Use this during or immediately after the call.

Program fit:

- Is the buyer looking for FAA Part 107, STEM/AI, media production, or a broader pathway?
- Is the school expecting a semester, full-year, club, short bootcamp, or workforce course?
- Are students high school, middle school, postsecondary, or adult learners?
- Does the program need teacher-led materials, async content, or both?

Implementation:

- How many students in year one?
- How many teachers or program managers?
- What start date matters?
- Do they already have drones or need hardware guidance?
- Do they need pacing guides, standards alignment, rubrics, or teacher resources before approval?

Buying process:

- Who owns the decision?
- Who controls budget?
- Is there a grant, CTE budget, Perkins path, PA SMART opportunity, or local budget?
- Is board approval required?
- Is a PO required?
- Is vendor onboarding required?
- Is legal, privacy, accessibility, or security review required?

Outcome:

- Qualified for quote now.
- Needs more information.
- Good fit, later cycle.
- Not a fit.

## Sales package checklist

Before sending a serious quote or proposal, confirm these items.

Quote/package:

- One-year vs three-year term selected.
- Unique invoice or quote number.
- Customer name, address, and contact are correct.
- Seat caps match the discussed program.
- Course names match the actual offer.
- Payment timing is clear.
- Pricing valid-through date is separate from subscription term.
- Sales tax/tax-exempt note is clear when applicable.
- Customer PO line is included if needed.

Contract:

- Appendix A exists and matches the quote.
- Subscription term is explicit.
- Offerings and seat caps match the quote.
- Renewal language is selected and unused options removed.
- AP, primary, and technical contacts are captured where possible.
- K-12 Terms of Use exhibit is included if referenced.

Legal/privacy/accessibility:

- Privacy link uses `https://thedroneedge.com/privacy`.
- Terms link uses `https://thedroneedge.com/legal`.
- School consent/student data questions are documented.
- DPA or subprocessors list request is tracked if raised.
- Accessibility/VPAT request is tracked if raised.

Operational:

- Org setup owner identified.
- Course assignments known.
- Teacher/manager accounts known.
- Student invite approach known.
- Launch date and onboarding call identified.

## Automation roadmap

Automation should help with recordkeeping, reminders, and consistency before it touches messaging decisions. Keep prospect-specific claims and final sends human-reviewed until there is enough response data and compliance confidence.

### Manual baseline

Keep these human-owned at first:

- Contact research and source verification.
- List approval.
- Email personalization and final send approval.
- Reply interpretation.
- Scheduling.
- Qualification.
- Quote/proposal preparation.
- Legal/procurement responses.

### Easy automation

These are good first scripts or lightweight tools:

- CSV cleanup and field normalization.
- Deduping by email, domain, and organization.
- Required-field checks before a contact is marked ready.
- Mail-merge draft generation from approved templates.
- Follow-up date reminders.
- Link bundle generation by segment.
- Simple dashboard from spreadsheet statuses.
- Canned reply, scheduling, and post-call recap templates.
- Export of weekly metrics.

### Moderate automation

Consider these after the manual process is stable:

- Spreadsheet to CRM import/export sync.
- Consultation form submissions copied into the tracker.
- Scheduled follow-up queues.
- Public directory enrichment for school, district, state, and role fields.
- Campaign-level reporting by state, role, segment, and source.
- Basic duplicate detection across campaigns.
- Reminder emails to the internal owner when a next step is overdue.

### Harder automation

Do these only when volume and process maturity justify the cost:

- Reliable contact discovery at scale.
- Deliverability-safe outbound sending infrastructure.
- Unsubscribe and compliance management.
- Personalized sequence branching based on replies and role.
- Calendar booking integration with availability rules.
- CRM pipeline ownership and task assignment.
- Proposal and quote generation from opportunity data.
- District procurement tracking.
- Attribution from email to consultation to closed deal.
- Automated lead scoring from website behavior.

## Risks and decisions

| Risk / decision | Why it matters | Current approach |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Manual vs service | Building too early can encode the wrong process. | Start with spreadsheet/manual workflow; automate proven repetitive steps. |
| Contact quality | Bad lists hurt deliverability and reputation. | Research smaller batches and keep source URLs. |
| Outreach compliance | Schools may require opt-outs or formal vendor paths. | Honor do-not-contact requests immediately; keep outreach targeted and professional. |
| Overclaiming readiness | Competitors often market pass rates and guarantees; unverified claims create trust risk. | Be precise about Part 107, STEM, media, question banks, and outcomes. |
| Grant eligibility | Funding rules vary by state, district, and RFA. | Share funding context, not guarantees; defer to grants/business offices. |
| Privacy and student data | School buyers may need FERPA/COPPA/DPA review. | Track requests and use the live Privacy Notice and contract package. |
| Accessibility | Contracts reference good-faith WCAG efforts and possible VPAT requests. | Track as a sales-readiness gap until a current packet exists. |
| Procurement package | Quotes can stall if Appendix A, PO, AP, or tax-exempt details are missing. | Use the sales package checklist before sending formal documents. |
| Missing sales assets | Serious buyers often want PDFs, standards maps, and pacing guides. | Use website links now; log repeated requests as collateral priorities. |
| Product roadmap gaps | SSO, roster sync, LMS/LTI, and deeper reporting may come up. | Present current org/manager features honestly and track roadmap demand. |

## Definition of done for the first campaign

The first campaign is successful if it produces useful market signal, not only immediate sales.

Minimum useful output:

- At least 100 researched contacts across one clear segment.
- All contacts have source URLs and statuses.
- First-touch and follow-up messages tested.
- Reply rate and meeting rate recorded.
- Common objections summarized.
- Missing collateral ranked by how often prospects ask for it.
- Any qualified opportunity has a clean next step, owner, and date.
