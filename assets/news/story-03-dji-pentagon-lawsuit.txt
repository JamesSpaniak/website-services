TITLE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DJI challenges U.S. “Chinese military company” listing and related federal restrictions (2024)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SUMMARY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Shenzhen DJI, the world’s largest civil drone vendor, contested U.S. government listings that connect the firm to Chinese military interests—impacting federal procurement and reputational risk in Western markets. The dispute sits at the intersection of civil-market branding, export control politics, and strategic competition.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
KEY INSIGHTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Legal fights highlight how “military company” labels can act like secondary sanctions in practice.
- Civil/military dual-use ambiguity is central: cameras, autonomy stacks, and supply chains overlap.
- Global customers face compliance screens beyond FAA-style airworthiness.
