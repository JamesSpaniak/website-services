TITLE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EU drone rules and EASA frameworks mature: classes, registration, and operator categories (2020s)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SUMMARY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Europe standardized drone operations through risk-based categories (Open/Specific/Certified), harmonizing many national quirks. Registration, remote pilot competency, and geo-awareness features became mainstream requirements for mid-sized drones, influencing global manufacturers’ product design.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
KEY INSIGHTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- EU approach ties operation type to aircraft capability and operational risk.
- UK post-Brexit regimes diverged but remained conceptually similar in places.
- CE marking and class labels shaped consumer drone SKUs sold worldwide.

PUBLIC OPINION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
European hobbyists debated burden vs. safety; industry generally preferred one harmonized rulebook over 27 fragmented ones. Environmental voices occasionally linked drones to wildlife disturbance in protected areas.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
